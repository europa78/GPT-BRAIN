**GPT:** **Understanding the Florida Document Number and FEI/EIN Number**  

When registering a business in Florida, you may come across two important identifiers:

1. **Florida Document Number**  
2. **FEI (Federal Employer Identification) / EIN (Employer Identification Number)**  

Below is an overview of what they are, why you might need them, and how to obtain them.

---

## 1. Florida Document Number

**What is it?**  
- The Florida Document Number is a unique tracking number assigned by the Florida Department of State’s Division of Corporations.  
- This number is used to identify your business entity (e.g., LLC, corporation) on the Sunbiz.org website, which is the official registry for Florida business entities.

**Why do I need it?**  
- It’s required for official state filings, annual reports, and any amendments or renewals you make to your business registration.  
- You may also need to provide this number when applying for certain local or state licenses or permits.

**How do I get it?**  
1. **Register Your Business with Florida:**  
   - You apply online through [Sunbiz.org](https://dos.myflorida.com/sunbiz/) to form your LLC or corporation.  
   - Once the registration is approved, the Division of Corporations will assign your business a Florida Document Number.  
2. **Locate Your Number Online:**  
   - If you have already registered and don’t know your document number, you can search for your company on [Sunbiz.org](https://search.sunbiz.org/). Enter the business name, and your assigned Florida Document Number will be visible in the search results.

---

## 2. FEI/EIN Number

**What is it?**  
- The **FEI Number (Federal Employer Identification Number)** and **EIN (Employer Identification Number)** are essentially the same thing—both terms refer to the nine-digit ID assigned by the IRS to a business entity.  
- It’s used for federal tax filing and identification purposes.

**Why do I need it?**  
- You must use your EIN on tax returns, payroll forms, and other official documentation.  
- An EIN is often required by banks when opening a business bank account, by vendors if you are establishing lines of credit, and by certain government agencies for licensing.

**How do I get it?**  
1. **Apply Online with the IRS:**  
   - The fastest way is to use the IRS **EIN Assistant** on their [website](https://www.irs.gov/businesses/small-businesses-self-employed/how-to-apply-for-an-ein).  
   - Online applications usually provide you an EIN instantly, assuming all questions are answered correctly.  
2. **Apply by Mail or Fax:**  
   - You can submit **Form SS-4** to the IRS by mail or fax, though processing times are longer than the online method.  
3. **International Applicants:**  
   - If you’re applying from outside the U.S., you can call the IRS directly. See the IRS website for international EIN instructions.

---

## Frequently Asked Questions

1. **Is the Florida Document Number the same as the EIN?**  
   - **No.** The Florida Document Number is used by the Florida Department of State, while the EIN is a federal identifier used by the IRS.

2. **Do I need both for my Florida-based business?**  
   - Typically, **yes**—most businesses formed in Florida need a Florida Document Number (to show they are a valid, registered entity in the state) and an EIN (to handle federal tax reporting and other business transactions).

3. **What if I’m a sole proprietor?**  
   - If you are operating under your legal name only (with no employees), you may not be required to get an EIN. However, many sole proprietors still choose to get an EIN to keep their personal Social Security Number private. Sole proprietors also do not get a Florida Document Number unless they form a separate legal entity like an LLC.

4. **Does obtaining these numbers cost money?**  
   - **Florida Document Number:** You pay a filing fee when you register your Florida business, but there’s no separate charge for the document number.  
   - **EIN:** It’s **free** to apply for an EIN via the IRS website or by mail/fax.

---

## Summary
- **Florida Document Number**: Issued by the Division of Corporations in Florida; identifies your business at the state level.  
- **FEI/EIN Number**: Issued by the IRS; identifies your business at the federal level for tax and payroll purposes.
