## The “Investor ABM” multi-channel cadence (12 touches / ~14 business days)

**Goal:** book a 10–15 min intro *or* get a clear “not a fit / later / send deck.”

### Day 1 (Mon/Tue AM) — Email #1 (personalized)
**Subject:** “[Fund/Name] × [YourCo]?”
- 1 line why them (portfolio / thesis / geo / stage)
- 2 bullets: your wedge + proof (traction / distribution / unfair advantage)
- CTA: “Worth sending a tight 1-pager?”

**Salesloft step:** Email

### Day 1 (same day) — LinkedIn “research touch”
View profile, follow, engage with 1 recent post (light touch, no pitch).

**Salesloft step:** Other (or LinkedIn step if you use the integration)

### Day 2 — LinkedIn connect request (short note)
“Quick hello — emailed you re: [very short thesis]. If I’m off, all good.”

**Salesloft step:** LinkedIn Sales Navigator step (Connect)

### Day 3 — Call #1 (no heavy pitch)
If they answer: confirm you emailed + ask permission to send 1-pager.
If voicemail: 10 seconds, reference the email + CTA (“ok to send 1-pager?”).

**Salesloft step:** Phone

### Day 4 — Email #2 (value / proof)
Subject: “Re: [Your wedge]”
- add 1 new datapoint (customer, usage, retention, partnership, waitlist, etc.)
- CTA: “Should I send the 1-pager or the deck?”

**Salesloft step:** Email

### Day 6 — LinkedIn message OR InMail (only if appropriate)
Keep it to one sentence + the same micro-CTA.

**Salesloft step:** LinkedIn Sales Navigator step (Message/InMail)

### Day 7 — Call #2 (no voicemail this time)
Quick attempt; if no answer, move on (don’t overcall investors).

**Salesloft step:** Phone

### Day 8 — Email #3 (video optional)
A short Loom/Vidyard intro can work well as the “humanizing” touch. Salesloft itself cites that adding a video (or direct mail) step can materially lift success rates. citeturn0search9

**Salesloft step:** Email (mention video) or Integration step (if you use Vidyard)

### Day 10 — Email #4 (referral / routing)
Subject: “Wrong person?”
“If you’re not the right contact for [stage/sector], who is?”

**Salesloft step:** Email

### Day 12 — Call #3 (optional, only for high-priority targets)
If you have real fit + signals (opens/clicks), do one last attempt.

**Salesloft step:** Phone

### Day 14 — Email #5 (“close the loop”)
Subject: “Close the loop?”
“Should I (a) send 1-pager, (b) circle back in 90 days, or (c) stop reaching out?”

**Salesloft step:** Email

## Why this cadence works best for fundraising
- It’s **ABM-style** (fewer prospects, more relevance), which matches how investors decide.
- It uses Salesloft’s **true multi-channel workflow** (email + calls + LinkedIn steps) without being obnoxious. citeturn0search1turn0search13
- It keeps the ask **small and binary**, which boosts replies (even “no” is progress).

## Salesloft setup tips that move the needle
- **Create 2 versions of the cadence by persona** (VC Partner vs Analyst/Associate vs Family Office). Same structure, different proof + CTA.
- **Use Templates + Snippets** for your core, but force personalization on Email #1 (and ideally #2).
- **Throttle automation**: Salesloft itself warns automated email steps should be used cautiously and only when dynamic fields are accurate. citeturn0search6
- **Only add SMS if you have consent** (investor outreach + texting can backfire fast).
