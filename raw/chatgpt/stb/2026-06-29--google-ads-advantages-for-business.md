How it works
A featured video in a Masthead ad autoplays without the sound for up to 30 seconds at the top of the YouTube Home feed. After autoplay, the main video defaults to the video thumbnail. When people click on the video or click on the thumbnail, they’re taken to the YouTube watch page for the video.



If you’re booking Masthead ads, use the Masthead Preview Tool to draft and finalize the Masthead creative before submitting it to Google.

How you'll be charged
Because Masthead ads are only available on a reservation basis, you’re charged on a cost-per-thousand-impressions (CPM) basis. You can work with the Google advertising team to get a rate estimate and campaign impression goals.



The auction buying experience
Skippable in-stream, bumpers, non-skippable in-stream and in-feed video ads are available using the auction buying experience. Auction is a flexible buying experience for those who want dynamic and efficient pricing. 



How does it work? Google Ads determines which ads should show with a lightning-fast ad auction, which takes place every time someone searches on Google or visits a site that shows ads, like YouTube.



Masthead ads and other formats are purchased on a reservation basis. Check out this Google Ads Help Center article to learn more about reserved media placements on YouTube.
