**Mike:** Summarize the main points of quantum mechanics in layman's terms.

**GPT:** Quantum mechanics is a branch of physics that deals with the behavior of particles at incredibly small scales, like atoms and subatomic particles. The main points include:

1. Wave-particle duality: Particles, such as electrons, can exhibit both wave-like and particle-like properties depending on how they are observed.

2. Superposition: Particles can exist in multiple states or locations simultaneously until they are measured, at which point they "collapse" into a single state or location.

3. Quantum entanglement: When two particles become entangled, the properties of one instantly affect the other, even if they are separated by vast distances.

4. Uncertainty principle: It's impossible to know a particle's exact position and momentum simultaneously. The more precisely one is known, the less precisely the other can be determined.

5. Quantum tunneling: Particles can pass through barriers that would be impossible to overcome in classical physics, due to their wave-like nature.

In summary, quantum mechanics reveals a world of counterintuitive behavior and interconnectedness at the smallest scales, challenging our classical understanding of physics.
