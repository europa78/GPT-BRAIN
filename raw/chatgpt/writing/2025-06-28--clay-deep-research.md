## Claygents: AI Agents for Research Automation

A standout feature of Clay is its **AI-driven research agents**, called **Claygents**. A Claygent is essentially an AI-powered web assistant that can find answers to questions or gather specific data by reading online sources – functioning like a tireless virtual SDR researcher. Claygents were built by integrating large language models (LLMs) such as OpenAI’s GPT-4 into the Clay platform【30†L123-L130】. The result is an agent that can **“research anything”** given a prompt, visiting websites to retrieve and summarize information much faster (and cheaper) than a human could【30†L123-L130】.

**How Claygents work:** In Clay’s interface, a user can add a Claygent as an enrichment step and give it an instruction or prompt. For example, you might instruct, *“For each company in this list, find out if they have a SOC 2 compliance page on their site and summarize what it says,”* or *“List any competitors mentioned on the company’s homepage.”* When run, Claygent will automatically navigate to the company’s website (and other relevant web pages if needed), use AI to read and understand the content, and then return the answer in your Clay table【2†L228-L236】【39†L216-L220】. Under the hood, the Claygent uses the LLM to decide where to look and to interpret the data. (For instance, GPT-4 might tell Claygent that “SOC 2 info is usually in the footer or security page,” so Claygent will target those areas【30†L159-L168】.) This intelligent crawling approach means Claygent isn’t just blindly scraping—it's actually “reading” like a human researcher and extracting the key points you asked for. It can handle both **company data queries** (e.g. product offerings, recent news, hiring trends) and **people data** queries (e.g. finding if a person was mentioned in press or what podcast a CEO was on recently)【2†L248-L256】【2†L258-L266】.
