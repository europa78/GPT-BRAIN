## Progress, innovation, and emergent order (Ridley / Harari / complexity)
- **The Beginning of Infinity** — David Deutsch — optimism-through-explanation; why progress can be unbounded.  
- **Enlightenment Now** — Steven Pinker — data-heavy case for progress (the “Rational Optimist” lane).  
- **Factfulness** — Hans Rosling — reality-check statistics + why things are often improving.  
- **Where Good Ideas Come From** — Steven Johnson — innovation as an ecosystem, not a lone genius.  
- **The Box** — Marc Levinson — globalization explained through the shipping container (quietly mind-blowing).  
- **Seeing Like a State** — James C. Scott — why top-down plans fail and bottom-up order wins.  
- **Complexity** — M. Mitchell Waldrop — the story of complexity science and emergence.  
- **The Origin of Wealth** — Eric D. Beinhocker — evolution applied to economics/innovation.

## Risk, markets, and “how the game actually works” (Taleb / Thorp / Dalio / Simons)
- **The Black Swan** — Nassim Nicholas Taleb — if you liked *Fooled by Randomness*, this is the next escalation.

- **Against the Gods** — Peter L. Bernstein — the history of risk (great bridge from Thorp → modern finance).  
- **More Money Than God** — Sebastian Mallaby — hedge fund history, strategies, and personalities.

- **When Genius Failed** — Roger Lowenstein — LTCM and leverage (a must if you like market structure stories).  
- **Reminiscences of a Stock Operator** — Edwin Lefèvre — timeless trading psychology.  
- **The Psychology of Money** — Morgan Housel — practical, narrative-driven finance reasoning.
