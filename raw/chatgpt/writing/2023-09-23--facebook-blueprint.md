**Mike:** List the different bid strategies

**GPT:** Bid strategies determine how your ad budget is spent in online advertising campaigns, especially on platforms like Facebook, Google Ads, and others. Here are the primary bid strategies used across these platforms:

1. **Lowest Cost (Automatic Bidding)**:
   - The system automatically sets your bid to get the most results at the lowest possible cost.

2. **Target Cost**:
   - This strategy aims to maintain an average cost as close to the target cost as possible. It's often used for campaigns with the objective of conversions or app installs.

3. **Cost Cap**:
   - It sets a maximum cost for the desired action (e.g., conversion). The system will try to get as many actions as possible without exceeding this cap.

4. **Bid Cap**:
   - You set a maximum bid for your ads, and the system will not bid more than this amount for any auction. This gives you more control but might limit the reach and results of your ads.

5. **Enhanced Cost-Per-Click (ECPC)**:
   - Used in Google Ads, ECPC adjusts your manual bids to try to maximize conversions. It can raise your bid in situations where a conversion seems more likely and lower it where a conversion seems less likely.

6. **Maximize Conversions**:
   - This strategy automatically sets bids to get the most conversions for your budget.

7. **Maximize Conversion Value**:
   - It sets bids to maximize the total conversion value while spending your budget.

8. **Target Return on Ad Spend (ROAS)**:
   - You set a target return on ad spend, and the system will set bids to achieve this return across all campaigns.

9. **Maximize Clicks**:
   - An automated strategy that sets your bid to get as many clicks as possible within your budget.

10. **Target Impression Share**:
   - This strategy aims to show your ad on a percentage of eligible ad impressions, either at the absolute top of the page, anywhere in the top section, or anywhere on the page.

11. **Target Search Page Location**:
   - It aims to show your ad at the top of the search results or on the first search results page.

12. **Target Outranking Share**:
   - This strategy aims to outrank another domain's ads in the search results.

Different platforms might have variations or additional bid strategies, and the best strategy often depends on the specific goals of the campaign. Advertisers should test and adjust their bid strategies based on performance data to optimize their campaigns effectively.
