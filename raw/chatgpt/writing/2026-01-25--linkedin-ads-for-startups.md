Here are the practical pros/cons on LinkedIn:

## CPC bidding (pay per click)
**Pros**
- **Direct-response friendly:** Best when your goal is **“Investor brief viewed,” “Deck request,” “Book a call”**.
- **More efficient when CTR is uncertain:** If you’re not sure your creative will get clicks, CPC protects you from paying for lots of impressions that don’t move anyone.
- **Great for small, premium audiences:** With family offices, you often want fewer—but **higher-quality**—visits.

**Cons**
- **Higher cost per click in investor-ish audiences:** Seniority + finance targeting tends to be expensive.
- **Can throttle delivery:** If LinkedIn predicts low click-likelihood (or your bid is conservative), you may get **limited impressions**.
- **Optimizes toward “clickers,” not “buyers”:** Some people click a lot but aren’t decision-makers. You have to qualify on-page (brief structure, proof, soft gating, etc.).

**Best use for you**
- Retargeting (site visitors/video viewers) → **CPC** to a 1-page investor brief or “request deck” flow.

## CPM bidding (pay per 1,000 impressions)
**Pros**
- **Maximizes reach in tight audiences:** Great for making sure the right titles *see* your story even if they don’t click immediately.
- **Perfect for building retargeting pools:** Video views, page visits, and engagement audiences get cheaper faster with CPM.
- **Better control over visibility:** If you need repetition (credibility, recognition), CPM is the lever.

**Cons**
- **You pay even if nobody clicks:** If your creative/offer isn’t sharp, CPM burns budget quietly.
- **Easy to overserve a small audience:** With US-only + niche targeting, frequency can spike (same people see you too much).
- **Harder to judge “quality” early:** You’ll see impressions/CTR, but investor conversion is later and noisier.

**Best use for you**
- Top-of-funnel credibility (short founder clip, traction proof, thesis) → **CPM** to generate qualified awareness and retargeting signal.

## The simple math that decides it
If your **CTR** is strong, CPM can be cheaper than CPC.

- Effective CPC from CPM ≈ **CPM ÷ (1000 × CTR)**
Example: CPM $80 and CTR 0.6% (0.006)  
Effective CPC ≈ 80 ÷ (1000×0.006) = 80 ÷ 6 = **$13.33/click**

So:
- If you think CTR will be **low (<0.4%)**, **CPC** is usually safer.
- If you have proven creative and CTR **≥0.6–1.0%**, **CPM** often wins.
