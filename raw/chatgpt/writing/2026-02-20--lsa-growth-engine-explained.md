## Explain it in 10 steps (college-student level)

1. **Start with the goal:** This is a system for growing **LSA (Local Services Ads)** performance by improving lead quality, conversion, and reputation—mostly through automation.

2. **Pick *where* you want leads from (Geo-Fencing):** Instead of targeting a whole city, you draw **custom shapes** on a map to focus on neighborhoods more likely to produce higher-paying customers.

3. **Filter for “A-player” clients:** The geo-fence is designed to attract better-fit customers (higher income, higher intent), which typically means better job value and fewer tire-kickers.

4. **Accept that some leads will still be bad:** Even with targeting, you’ll get wrong-number, spam, or low-quality inquiries. The system plans for this.

5. **Automatically recover money on junk leads:** **Automated Revenue Recovery** means the system flags unqualified leads and files for refunds so your ad spend isn’t wasted.

6. **Train the “algorithm” with real data:** The **AI Algorithm Training** step uses post-lead or post-call surveys to capture details (lead quality, job type, value, outcome). That data becomes feedback.

7. **Use that feedback to attract higher-value jobs:** Over time, the system “teaches” what a good lead looks like, so marketing and intake get aligned around quality—not just volume.

8. **Boost reputation automatically (Review Autopilot):** After a job is finished, an AI-driven follow-up (calls/messages) prompts happy customers to leave **5-star text reviews** fast.

9. **Improve your intake team in real time:** **Real-Time Intake Feedback** gives coaching signals (quality control) so phone handling improves—raising booking rates and reducing missed opportunities.

10. **Connect everything directly to Google (API Integration):** Instead of manually entering or moving data around, **Direct API Integration** pushes key info to Google systems, reducing friction and making scaling easier and more consistent.
