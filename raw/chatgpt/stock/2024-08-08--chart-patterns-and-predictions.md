### Reversal Patterns
1. **Double Top**
   - **Description:** This pattern forms after an uptrend and is a bearish reversal pattern. It resembles an "M" shape where the price hits a high, retraces, rises again to a similar level, and then declines.
   - **Prediction:** Indicates a potential reversal from an uptrend to a downtrend. The price might drop significantly after breaking the neckline.

2. **Head and Shoulders**
   - **Description:** This is another bearish reversal pattern. It consists of three peaks: a higher peak (head) between two lower peaks (shoulders).
   - **Prediction:** Suggests a reversal from bullish to bearish trend. A price decline is expected after breaking the neckline.

3. **Rising Wedge**
   - **Description:** This pattern slopes upward and can indicate either a continuation or a reversal depending on its context within a trend.
   - **Prediction:** Generally considered bearish, especially if it forms after an uptrend, indicating a potential reversal to a downtrend.

4. **Double Bottom**
   - **Description:** The opposite of a double top, forming a "W" shape. It's a bullish reversal pattern that occurs after a downtrend.
   - **Prediction:** Signals a potential reversal from a downtrend to an uptrend. A significant price rise is expected after breaking the neckline.

5. **Inverse Head and Shoulders**
   - **Description:** This is a bullish reversal pattern and the opposite of the head and shoulders pattern.
   - **Prediction:** Indicates a change from a bearish to a bullish trend. Price is expected to rise significantly after breaking the neckline.

6. **Falling Wedge**
   - **Description:** Slopes downward and typically signifies a bullish reversal when occurring after a downtrend.
   - **Prediction:** Suggests that the price may increase after breaking out of the wedge.

### Continuation Patterns
1. **Falling Wedge**
   - **Description:** This pattern appears during an uptrend and indicates that the trend will likely continue upward.
   - **Prediction:** Bullish continuation pattern, suggesting the price will rise after breakout.

2. **Bullish Rectangle**
   - **Description:** A consolidation period where the price moves sideways between two horizontal levels.
   - **Prediction:** Indicates the continuation of an uptrend once the price breaks above the rectangle's resistance level.

3. **Bullish Pennant**

   - **Prediction:** Bullish, suggesting the uptrend will resume after a breakout above the pennant.

   - **Description:** Typically seen as a bearish continuation pattern during a downtrend.
   - **Prediction:** Suggests that the price will continue to fall after the wedge breaks downward.

5. **Bearish Rectangle**
   - **Description:** Similar to a bullish rectangle but occurs during a downtrend.
   - **Prediction:** Indicates the continuation of a downtrend once the price breaks below the rectangle's support level.

6. **Bearish Pennant**
   - **Description:** A bearish continuation pattern formed after a sharp decline in price, with converging trendlines.
   - **Prediction:** Suggests further decline once the price breaks below the pennant.

### Bilateral Patterns
1. **Ascending Triangle**
   - **Description:** Characterized by a horizontal resistance line and an upward sloping support line.
   - **Prediction:** Generally bullish, indicating that the price may break out upwards, but can also be bearish if the price breaks below the support.

2. **Descending Triangle**
   - **Description:** Features a downward-sloping resistance line and a horizontal support line.
   - **Prediction:** Typically bearish, suggesting a downward breakout, though a breakout above the resistance can be bullish.

3. **Symmetrical Triangle**
   - **Description:** Consists of converging trendlines where the price is making lower highs and higher lows.
   - **Prediction:** Considered a neutral pattern that can break out in either direction; traders watch for a breakout to determine the trend.
