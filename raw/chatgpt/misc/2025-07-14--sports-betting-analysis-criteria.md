**Mike:** What is a sharp bettor?

**GPT:** A **sharp bettor** (also called a "pro" or "wiseguy") is someone who:

### ✅ Bets with an edge
They consistently find **value**—situations where the odds offered by the sportsbook are **mispriced** relative to the actual probability of the event. This gives them a long-term positive expected return (EV+).

---

### ✅ Traits of a Sharp Bettor:
1. **Data-driven**: Uses models, statistics, and historical trends.
2. **Disciplined**: Bets based on value, not emotion.
3. **Line-sensitive**: Places bets when odds offer the most value, often before the public moves the line.
4. **Bankroll management**: Uses strict unit sizing to survive swings (Kelly Criterion or flat-betting).
5. **Specialization**: Focuses on specific sports, leagues, or niche markets where they can spot inefficiencies.

---

### ✅ Key Differences from a Recreational Bettor ("Square"):
| Trait | Sharp Bettor | Recreational Bettor |
|------|---------------|----------------------|
| Goal | Long-term profit | Entertainment or short-term wins |
| Research | Deep modeling, data | News, gut feel, recent results |
| Market timing | Bets early (or late) to beat the line | Bets just before games |
| Edge | Small, consistent | Often none; may overbet favorites or parlays |
| Book limits | May get limited or banned | Almost never limited |

---

### 📌 Example:
Let’s say:
- The true probability of a team winning is **60%**, but the bookmaker is offering +120 odds (implied ~45%).
- A sharp instantly recognizes this **mispricing** and bets it heavily.
- A square might ignore it or even bet the other side because it “feels right.”

---

### ⚠️ Books React to Sharps
When a sharp bettor wagers:
- Books may **move the line** to reflect the sharp money.
- Some books track sharp action and adjust faster than others.
- A sharp bettor often tries to remain **unnoticed** to avoid having their limits cut.

---

In short:  
**Sharp bettors think like investors**, not gamblers. Their edge lies not in predicting outcomes perfectly, but in **betting when the price is wrong.**
