# Step 6: Common Cold-Outreach Mistakes to Avoid  
- **No personalization or segmentation:**  A generic blast kills engagement. Tailor your emails by business type, location, or known pain. Segmented campaigns get **75% more clicks** than non-segmented ones【30†L90-L98】. For home services, you might create separate cadences for plumbers vs. roofers, each speaking to that niche’s needs.  

- **Poor data & verification:**  Sending to bad contacts hurts deliverability. Avoid “cheap list” traps – empty or outdated addresses raise bounce rates and get you flagged【7†L117-L127】【30†L120-L129】. Use ZoomInfo to build quality lists and verify emails before launch.  

- **Weak or misleading subject lines:**  Refrain from clickbait or spammy phrases (e.g. “Best plumbers” or overusing “FREE”). As GlassHouse notes, the subject *must accurately reflect your message*【29†L224-L233】. Also, avoid words that trigger spam filters (“guaranteed”, excessive exclamation points, all caps)【21†L239-L247】【29†L224-L233】.  

- **Too many CTAs (or none):**  Including multiple calls-to-action (e.g. “Book now”, “Download here”, “Visit site”) in one email dilutes the focus. Stick to *one* clear CTA【19†L75-L83】. Likewise, having *no* CTA is bad – always tell them exactly what next step you want.  

- **No follow-up plan:**  Sending a single email and quitting is a huge mistake. Studies show over 80% of prospects never respond to the first email. Use follow-ups religiously. As noted, a typical B2B sale often needs 5+ touches【9†L158-L162】; not following up means missed opportunities.  

- **Ignoring compliance:**  Always follow legal rules (CAN-SPAM in the U.S.). Don’t falsify your header info or subject, and always include a clear unsubscribe link and your mailing address【29†L224-L233】【29†L240-L244】. Home service owners can be skittish about spam, so respecting their privacy builds trust.  

- **Too much self-talk:**  Avoid bombarding prospects with your company’s features. One common error is writing emails that sound all about *you* instead of *them*. Focus on their problems and goals. For example, instead of “Our agency has 10 years of experience,” say “You can start getting calls from new customers within 30 days.” The recipient should feel you understand their world, not that they just got a sales brochure.  

- **Poor timing:**  Don’t send emails at odd hours or on weekends. Midweek mornings (Tuesday–Thursday) often see higher engagement【1†L158-L161】. Also, respect time zones: Salesloft’s scheduling can help ensure emails arrive at appropriate local times.
