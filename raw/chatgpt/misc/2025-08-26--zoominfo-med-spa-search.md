Here’s what’s happening when your **Industry + Keywords** combo produces only a small number of MedSpas:  

---

## 🔎 Why the Results Shrink
1. **ZoomInfo Search = AND Logic by Default**  
   - When you select **Industry = Health & Wellness** *and* type keywords like `"MedSpa"` or `"Aesthetics"`, ZoomInfo only returns companies that match **both** filters simultaneously.  
   - That means: a company must be *classified under that industry* **and** have those keywords in its profile → huge drop-off.  

2. **Industry Tagging in ZoomInfo is Messy**  
   - Many MedSpas are tagged as:  
     - **Medical Practices**  
     - **Cosmetics**  
     - **Alternative Medicine**  
     - Even “Hospital & Health Care” or “Individual & Family Services.”  
   - If you restrict industry too tightly, you filter them *out*, even if they’re real MedSpas.  

3. **Keyword Coverage is Incomplete**  
   - Some MedSpas don’t even use “MedSpa” in their description — they may just say “Aesthetic Center” or “Skin Rejuvenation Clinic.”  
   - If your keyword net isn’t broad, you’ll miss them.  

---

## ⚡ How to Fix It (Best Practice)
### Method 1: Run **Separate Searches**
- **Search A:** Industry = Health/Wellness/Cosmetics/etc. (no keywords)  
- **Search B:** Keyword Boolean: `"MedSpa" OR "Medical Spa" OR "Aesthetics" OR "Anti-Aging" OR "Skin & Laser"` (no industry filter)  
- Export both → Deduplicate → Merge.  
👉 This way you capture MedSpas that *only* show up under keyword OR *only* show up under industry.  

### Method 2: Use **OR Logic with Multiple Industries**
- Instead of locking to one industry, select **several relevant industries** at once.  
- Then layer broad keywords → you’ll get much more coverage.  

### Method 3: Build a **Workflow with List Match**
- Take an external directory scrape (e.g., “Top 1,000 MedSpas in U.S.” from Google Maps or MedSpa Association).  
- Upload into ZoomInfo **List Match** → it enriches every one with owners + verified emails.  
- This bypasses the Industry/Keyword filter issue entirely.  

---

If you combine **Industry + Keywords**, you’re narrowing too much. ZoomInfo isn’t “wrong” — it’s just literal. To capture *every* MedSpa, you need to **separate searches, merge exports, and use List Match for coverage gaps.**
