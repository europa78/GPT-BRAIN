### 1. LinkedIn – Non-negotiable for this kind of firm

Given Greentree’s model—advising high-growth companies on uplistings, SPACs, PIPEs, SEC compliance, etc.citeturn0search0turn0search2—your buyers are founders, CEOs, CFOs, and boards. They live on LinkedIn.

**Why it’s top-2:**

- It’s *the* B2B network; targeting by title, industry, company size, geography.
- Perfect for credibility signals: transactions, client quotes, media, speaking, whitepapers.
- Direct path to conversations: DMs, InMail, connection requests, event invites.

**How to use it:**

- **Company Page**
  - Deal tombstones / “Recent Transactions” posts.
  - “How we helped X uplist to NASDAQ” style mini case studies (no confidentials).
  - Short educational posts:  
    - “3 SEC filing mistakes that delay uplistings”  
    - “What boards misunderstand about PIPE terms”
  - Community / philanthropy content (like Greentree’s Haiti / community work) to humanize the brand.citeturn0search6  

- **Leadership Profiles (this is huge)**
  - CEO / President and senior advisors posting weekly:
    - Market / regulatory commentary.
    - Breakdowns of SPAC/IPO headlines in plain English.
    - Behind-the-scenes of guiding a company through a listing (at a high level).
  - Use featured sections to link to:
    - Website service pages.
    - Long-form articles or videos.
    - Webinar replays.

- **Paid LinkedIn (optional but powerful)**
  - Sponsored thought-leadership posts targeting:
    - Job titles: CEO, CFO, General Counsel, Head of IR.
    - Company size: revenue / employee range that fits your ideal client.
  - Lead gen forms for:
    - “IPO Readiness Checklist”

### 2. YouTube – Deep-trust, complex-topic machine

**Why it’s top-2:**

- Long-form video builds way more trust than short posts.
- Perfect for explaining IPO, SPAC, PIPE, PCAOB issues in a visual, step-by-step way.

  - Website embeds (on gtfinancial.com-style pages).citeturn0search0

## Week 1 – Positioning & Credibility

### Day 1 (Mon) – Company Page  
**Goal:** Clearly state who you are, who you serve, and why.  
**Format:** Image + Text (hero banner / office / team photo)

- **Hook:**  
  “Most founders underestimate how early they should start preparing to go public.”  
- **Content Idea:**  
  - 2–3 sentences on what your firm does (IPO/SPAC/uplisting, capital formation, SEC advisory).  
  - Bullet out: “Who we work with” (revenue range, sectors, geographies).  
  - Short 3-step “How we engage”: Discovery → Diagnostics → Execution.  
- **CTA:**  
  “Send us a message or comment ‘Readiness’ for our IPO/Uplist Readiness Checklist.”

---

### Day 3 (Wed) – Founder/Managing Partner Profile  
**Goal:** Humanize leadership, introduce story & point of view.  
**Format:** Text-only or headshot + text

- **Hook:**  
  “After 20+ years in capital markets, here’s what most CEOs get wrong about ‘timing the market.’”  
- **Content Idea:**  
  - Short origin story: why you started the firm, what gap you saw.  
  - One lesson: e.g. “Perfect market timing is less important than internal readiness (audits, governance, story).”  
  - Tie to current macro environment (without giving securities advice).  
- **CTA:**  
  “If you’re thinking about an uplist or capital event in the next 24 months, connect with me here on LinkedIn.”

---

### Day 5 (Fri) – Company Page  
**Goal:** Social proof / credibility  
**Format:** Deal “tombstone” style graphic + short caption

- **Hook:**  
  “Another high-growth company successfully uplisted to a senior exchange.”  
- **Content Idea:**  
  - Share anonymized or already-public win: sector, size, what you helped with (no confidentials).  
  - Format: “Challenge → What we did → Outcome.”  
- **CTA:**  
  “Want to understand what this process would look like for your company? Message us ‘Case Study’.”

---

## Week 2 – Educational Deep Dives (Capital Markets & Process)

### Day 8 (Mon) – Company Page  
**Goal:** Educate on core service (e.g., uplisting)  
**Format:** Doc/Carousel post (“swipeable slides”)

- **Hook:**  
  “Thinking about uplisting to NASDAQ or NYSE? Start here.”  
- **Content Idea (slides):**  
  1. Title: “Your 7-Step Uplisting Roadmap”  
  2. Governance & board structure  
  3. Financial audits & reporting standards  
  4. Capital structure & float  
  5. SEC filings & timelines  
  6. Investor story & IR foundation  
  7. “Common pitfalls & how we help clients avoid them”  
- **CTA:**  
  “Comment ‘Roadmap’ and we’ll send you a PDF version.”

---

### Day 10 (Wed) – Partner / Senior Advisor Profile  
**Goal:** Show expertise in a specific niche (SPAC, PIPE, etc.)

- **Hook:**

- **Content Idea:**  
  - 3 bullet “When a SPAC makes sense / doesn’t make sense.”

- **CTA:**  
  “If your board is debating SPAC vs. traditional IPO, DM me ‘Compare’ and I’ll send a decision framework we use with clients.”

### Day 12 (Fri) – Company Page

- **Options:**  
  - “SEC / regulatory complexity”  
  - “Cost & fees”  
  - “Market conditions / valuation risk”

## Week 3 – Risk, Compliance & “Avoid the Landmines”

### Day 15 (Mon) – Company Page  
**Goal:** Show you protect them from expensive mistakes  
**Format:** Text post or image + text

- **Hook:**  
  “The most expensive part of going public is usually not the fees—it’s the mistakes.”  
- **Content Idea:**  
  List 3–5 “silent killers” in SEC/IPO readiness:  
  - Weak internal controls & documentation  
  - Last-minute scrambling on PCAOB audits  
  - Inconsistent messaging across S-1, press, and website  
  - Underestimating IR/communications workload  
- **CTA:**  
  “If you want a quick ‘red flag review’ before you go too far down the path, message us ‘Red Flags’.”

---

### Day 17 (Wed) – Founder Profile

**Format:** Short story post

- **Hook:**

- **CTA:**  
  “If you’re growing fast and know governance is a weak spot, connect with me and let’s talk.”
