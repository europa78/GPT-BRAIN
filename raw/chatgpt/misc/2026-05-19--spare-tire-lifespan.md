**GPT:** It depends on the type of spare tire you have. Most modern cars use a **temporary “donut” spare**, which is only meant to get you to a repair shop.

## Temporary Donut Spare

Typical limits:

- **Maximum distance:** about **50–70 miles**
- **Maximum speed:** usually **50 mph**
- Avoid:
  - long highway driving
  - heavy loads
  - aggressive braking/cornering
  - driving in bad weather if possible

Many donut spares have the exact limits printed on the sidewall.

Why the limit:
- smaller size changes handling and braking
- less tread and weaker construction
- can stress the differential/transmission
- reduced traction

If your vehicle has a **full-size spare** that matches your normal tires:
- You can usually drive on it much longer
- But it still should be treated as temporary if:
  - tread depth is very different
  - tire age is old
  - it’s a different brand/model

## Important Thing Most People Miss: Spare Tire Age
Even if never used, spare tires age out.

A spare sitting in the trunk for 10+ years may be unsafe at highway speeds. Check:
- cracks or dry rot
- low air pressure
- manufacturing date (DOT code)

Most tire experts recommend replacing tires around:
- **6–10 years old**, depending on condition
