* **Build an ensemble:** Combine a transparent baseline (e.g., Elo) with at least one pitcher-centric layer (Stuff+ or Bayesian) and a run-distribution engine (Poisson or Monte Carlo). Averaging or stacking usually trims mean absolute error by 2-4 percentage points vs any solo model.

## Guardrails & best practices

1. **Ensure orthogonality.**  
   *If two models both embed xFIP, down-weight one or strip duplicate features.*

2. **Walk-forward validation.**  
   Re-train on rolling three-year windows, test on the next six weeks; this mirrors betting reality better than random CV.

3. **Regular feature pruning.**  
   Drop stale signals (e.g., sprint-speed if it stops adding lift) instead of endlessly adding complexity.

4. **Bet-size with fractional Kelly on ensemble edge**, never on a single sub-model—this naturally scales stake when models align and shrinks it when they diverge.

5. **Track correlation of misses.**  
   If two modules start losing together (ρ > 0.7 on error), investigate overlap or data drift.

| **Quick openers Sunday night** | Lightweight Elo-starter | Lines soft, speed > depth. |
