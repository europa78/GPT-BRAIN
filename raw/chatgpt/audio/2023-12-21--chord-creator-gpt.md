### Perfect

The term "perfect" applies to intervals that historically were considered to be most consonant and stable, such as the unison, fourth, fifth, and octave. These intervals were seen as so harmonically pure that they didn't need to be classified as major or minor. In the perfect intervals, the notes blend so well that they are perceived as particularly stable and lacking tension. The term "perfect" also implies that these intervals have not been altered to become augmented or diminished.
