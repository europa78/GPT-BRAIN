**4. Audio-to-Audio Generation**

In audio-to-audio generation, you start with existing audio that the AI uses as a foundation, along with a text prompt to guide variations or transformations.

- **Overview:**  
  Upload, record, or select existing AI-generated audio to serve as “input audio.” Combined with your text prompt, this feature allows you to alter style, genre, or mood while retaining elements of the original sound.  
  *(See Audio-to-Audio Overview citeturn0file2)*

- **Types of Input Audio:**  
  - **Generate Audio:** Use previously created AI audio as a base.  
  - **Upload:** Bring in your own audio files (compatible formats include MP3, WAV, MP4, AIFF).  
  - **Record:** Use the built-in recording feature for quick audio capture.  
  - **Vocals:** Sing or hum to create quick backing tracks.

- **Creating Samples:**  
  - **Input Audio Strength:** Use the slider to control how much the input audio influences the output. A higher strength retains more of the original sound, while a lower strength permits greater transformation.  
  - **Examples:** The guide includes examples such as transforming a piano stem into different instrument sounds, generating varied versions of a heavy metal guitar sample, or creating sound effects like ringtones and explosions.

- **Monthly Allowance and Upload Details:**  
  - Depending on your subscription (Free, Pro, Studio, or Max), you have a set amount of audio you can upload each month.  
  - All uploaded audio is checked for copyright, cropped to a maximum length, and stored separately from AI-generated audio.
