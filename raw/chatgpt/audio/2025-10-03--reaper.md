### Tempo / Time Signature (excerpt)

> **Tempo / Time Signature Mapping & Timebase**  
>
> You can view, edit, and insert tempo and time signature markers on the timeline. These allow you to change tempo or meter at specific points in your project.  
>
> A **tempo map** is a timeline of tempo / meter changes — REAPER will interpolate or adjust between those points depending on project settings.  
>
> You control how media items (audio, MIDI) “follow” tempo / meter changes via the **project timebase** settings:  
> - “Beats (position, length, rate)” — items stretch/move with tempo changes  
> - “Time (position, length, rate)” — items stay fixed in time and do not stretch  
>
> To insert a tempo / time signature marker:  
> 1. Right‑click in the timeline (ruler)  
> 2. Choose **Insert tempo / time signature marker**  
>
> To show the tempo / time signature map:  
> - View → Master Track → click **Env** → enable **Tempo / Time Signature** envelope  
>
> The **transport bar / BPM field** is another way to edit global tempo / meter.  
> You can also use **tap tempo** via the BPM button in the transport.  
>
> When stretching items, REAPER uses different algorithms (e.g. preserving pitch or not) depending on the media type and stretching mode.
