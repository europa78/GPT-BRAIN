## 🎚 3. **Layering and Looping Process**

The team described their method like this:
1. **Find a groove** from an old record and loop it for about 5 minutes onto one tape track.  
2. **Find a second loop**, spend hours syncing it perfectly to the first, then print it to another track.  
3. Repeat for multiple loops (basslines, percussion, riffs, movie snippets).  
4. **Use automation** via the Commodore-linked mixing board to manually bring loops in and out — effectively “performing” the mix.  
This was painstaking, pre-digital editing that took “hours of trial and error.”【28†Paul’s Boutique†L2770-L2810】

---

## 🎧 4. **Division of Labor**

- **Matt Dike** – the “idea man” and sample digger; supplied source records, creative direction, and arrangement ideas, often phoning in instructions from home.  
- **Dust Brothers (Mike Simpson & John King)** – handled the **hands-on sampling, sequencing, and engineering**. They were described as “splitting the atom” with loops and timecode.  
- **Mario Caldato Jr.** – “the unsung hero,” engineer who made the sessions work technically; later became the Beasties’ go-to studio producer.  
- **Beastie Boys** – lyricists and co-producers; added vocal structure and humor, choosing to rap over dense, evolving beats instead of conventional drum loops【28†Paul’s Boutique†L1240-L1300】.
