3. **Trade Execution:**
   - **Sell Put Option:** Sell 1 JPM August 18, 2024, $145 Put
   - **Buy Put Option:** Buy 1 JPM August 18, 2024, $140 Put

**Strategy Explanation:**

1. **Sell Put Option ($145 Strike):**
   - Receive premium income from selling the $145 strike put option.
   - This obligates you to buy JPM shares at $145 if the option is exercised.

2. **Buy Put Option ($140 Strike):**
   - Pay premium for buying the $140 strike put option.
   - This provides protection by limiting potential losses if JPM falls below $140.

**Net Premium:**
- The net premium received is the difference between the premium collected from the sold put and the premium paid for the bought put.

**Maximum Profit:**
- The maximum profit is achieved if JPM remains above $145 at expiration.
- Maximum profit = Net premium received.

**Maximum Loss:**
- The maximum loss is limited to the difference between the strike prices minus the net premium received.
- Maximum loss = ($145 - $140) - Net premium received.

**Break-Even Point:**
- The break-even point is the strike price of the sold put minus the net premium received.
- Break-even point = $145 - Net premium received.

**Risk Management:**
- The bull put spread limits potential losses while allowing for profit from the premium received.
- This strategy takes advantage of the bullish outlook on JPM based on strong earnings and positive market sentiment.

**Trade Execution Example:**
Assuming the following premiums:
- Sell $145 Put: Receive $3.00 premium.
- Buy $140 Put: Pay $1.00 premium.
- Net premium received = $3.00 - $1.00 = $2.00.

**Trade Outcomes:**

1. **Maximum Profit:**
   - If JPM remains above $145 at expiration, both options expire worthless.
   - Maximum profit = $2.00 (net premium received) * 100 shares = $200 per contract.

2. **Maximum Loss:**
   - If JPM falls below $140 at expiration, the maximum loss occurs.
   - Maximum loss = ($145 - $140) - $2.00 = $3.00 * 100 shares = $300 per contract.

3. **Break-Even Point:**
   - Break-even point = $145 - $2.00 = $143.
