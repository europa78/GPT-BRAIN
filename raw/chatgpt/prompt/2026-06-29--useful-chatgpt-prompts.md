**Mike:** what are zero shot prompts ?

**GPT:** Zero-shot prompts are prompts that are given to a language model without any additional context or training data. The model is expected to generate a response based solely on its pre-trained knowledge and understanding of language.

Zero-shot prompts can be used to test the generalization ability of a language model, as well as its ability to generate coherent and meaningful responses to novel prompts. They can be particularly useful for evaluating the performance of language models on tasks that are outside of their original training domain.

For example, if a language model was trained on a large dataset of news articles, it might be able to generate coherent and informative responses to zero-shot prompts about current events or political issues. However, it might struggle to generate meaningful responses to prompts about highly specialized topics that were not covered in its training data.

Overall, zero-shot prompts can be a useful tool for evaluating the capabilities of a language model and identifying areas where it may need additional training or fine-tuning.
