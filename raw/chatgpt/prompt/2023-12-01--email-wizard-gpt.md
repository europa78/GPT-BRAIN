**GPT:** Timing the follow-up email after your initial outreach is crucial for maintaining engagement without being intrusive. Based on best practices in email marketing and sales outreach, here are some guidelines:

1. **Wait Period**: Typically, a follow-up email should be sent about 3-5 business days after the initial email. This timeframe allows the recipient enough time to read and consider your first email without feeling rushed, but it's also soon enough to keep your proposal fresh in their mind.

2. **Consider the Context**: Since your outreach is to a political candidate during an active campaign period, be mindful of their likely busy schedule. Campaign periods can be hectic, so providing a bit more time might be necessary.

3. **Content of Follow-Up**: Your second email should be a gentle reminder of your previous communication. It's important to be courteous and not too forceful. You can briefly reiterate your value proposition and express your eagerness to discuss how you can contribute to their campaign.

4. **Avoid Over-Following Up**: If there’s no response to your second email, it's advisable to wait for another week or two before sending a third email. Beyond that, consider limiting your follow-up attempts to avoid the risk of appearing pushy.

5. **Time of Day**: Send your follow-up email at a time when it's likely to be seen. Early in the workday (between 8 AM and 10 AM) or just after lunch (around 1 PM to 2 PM) can be effective times.

Remember, the key is persistence without being overbearing. Maintaining a professional and respectful tone throughout your communications is essential.
