**GPT:** ### Effective Altruism: An Overview

#### Definition
**Effective Altruism (EA)** is a philosophy and social movement that applies evidence and reason to determine the most effective ways to benefit others. It's not just about doing good; it's about maximizing the good one can do with their resources, be it time, money, or skills.

#### Key Principles
1. **Evidence-Based**: EA emphasizes using rigorous evidence to identify the most effective ways to help others.
2. **Cause Neutrality**: Rather than committing to a specific cause from the outset, it involves being open to supporting whatever cause or intervention turns out to be the most impactful.
3. **Cost-Effectiveness**: Focuses on the most efficient ways to use resources for the greatest positive impact.
4. **Global Perspective**: Often addresses global issues, considering the welfare of all beings, not just those in one's immediate community or country.
5. **Long-term Outlook**: Considers the long-term effects of actions and prioritizes interventions that have sustainable, lasting benefits.

#### Applications
- **Charity**: Determining which charities are most effective per dollar spent.
- **Career Choice**: Making career decisions based not just on personal benefit but on how one can most effectively contribute to societal good.
- **Research**: Focused on areas that have the potential for high impact, often neglected in mainstream discourse.

#### Criticisms and Challenges
- **Measurability Bias**: A tendency to favor causes where impact is more easily quantifiable, potentially overlooking important areas that are harder to measure.
- **Ethical Considerations**: Debates on how to weigh different types of benefits and harms, and the moral implications of different interventions.
- **Practicality**: Balancing idealism with practical constraints and the complexity of real-world problems.

#### Impact
- **Influencing Philanthropy**: EA has influenced high-net-worth individuals and philanthropic organizations in their giving strategies.
- **Shaping Careers**: Guiding individuals to seek careers that have significant positive impacts.
- **Raising Awareness**: Highlighting often neglected but crucial global issues.

#### Conclusion
Effective Altruism is a movement that challenges individuals and organizations to think critically and strategically about how to do the most good, combining a compassionate aim with a pragmatic approach.
