**Mike:** Best way to model financial statements?

**GPT:** The best way to model financial statements in Excel involves a few key steps:

1. **Data Gathering**: Collect historical financial data - Income Statement, Balance Sheet, and Cash Flow Statement.

2. **Structure Setup**: Create separate sheets for assumptions, income statement, balance sheet, and cash flow statement. Link them logically.

3. **Input Assumptions**: Define your assumptions (growth rates, margin targets, etc.) in a dedicated sheet. This makes the model flexible and easy to update.

4. **Build Income Statement**: Start with revenue, subtract costs to get gross profit, then operating expenses to arrive at operating profit. Account for interest, taxes to get net income.

5. **Construct Balance Sheet**: Link beginning and ending balances for assets, liabilities, and equity. Include formulas to reflect changes based on the income statement and cash flows.

6. **Develop Cash Flow Statement**: Start with net income, adjust for non-cash items, and account for changes in working capital. Include investing and financing activities.

7. **Link the Statements**: Ensure the net income from the income statement flows into the equity section of the balance sheet and the top of the cash flow statement.

8. **Scenario Analysis**: Use your assumptions sheet to test different scenarios and see their impact on financial statements.

9. **Validation**: Check for consistency and accuracy. Ensure the balance sheet balances and cash flows are correctly tied.

10. **Formatting and Documentation**: Use clear formatting for ease of reading and document your assumptions and sources.

Remember, the key is in how these statements interact. For instance, depreciation affects the income statement (as an expense) and the balance sheet (as a reduction in asset value), and is a non-cash adjustment in the cash flow statement.
