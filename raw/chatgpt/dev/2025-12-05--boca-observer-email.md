A few key points:

1. **Spam filters don’t nuke you for that phrase alone**  
   - It’s short  
   - No ALL CAPS  
   - No exclamation marks  
   - No obvious spam trigger words (“FREE!!!”, “Make money fast”, etc.)  
   So purely from a keyword standpoint, it’s not a guaranteed spam magnet.

2. **Bigger risks are reputation + engagement**  
   Deliverability is driven way more by:
   - Your sending domain / IP reputation  
   - Past open rates, reply rates, and spam complaints  
   - How many people delete or “Report spam” after seeing that subject  
   If “Print is dead” feels too click-baity or negative to some recipients, they’re more likely to ignore or complain, which **hurts you over time**.

3. **Your body copy matters too**  
   The version you shared earlier had:
   - Lots of ALL CAPS (“STOP WASTING”, “ANALYTICS AND ROI”)  
   - Strong language and big promises  
   - “FREE ad” + “We guarantee…”  
   Those elements combined are more concerning for filters than just “Print is dead” as a subject.
