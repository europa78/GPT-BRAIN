## E. Community engagement SOP

### 1) Response goals
The LinkedIn page should:
- respond promptly
- stay factual
- avoid speculation
- direct people to official resources
- escalate sensitive issues fast

### 2) Response time targets
- General comments: within 24 hours
- Investor questions: within 1 business day
- Misinformation or rumor posts: same day
- Media or analyst inquiries: escalate immediately

### 3) Who handles what
- **Marketing/social team:** routine replies, moderation, routing
- **IR lead:** investor-specific questions
- **Legal/compliance:** sensitive wording, rumors, forward-looking risk
- **Executive office:** only if approved and necessary

### 4) Approved response types

**A. Positive comment**  
“Thank you for following Avalon and for your interest. We appreciate your support and encourage you to follow our page for future public updates.”

**B. General question about the company**  
“Thanks for your question. For official company information, including press releases and investor materials, please visit [IR_URL].”

**C. Platform question**  
“Thanks for the question. We can only reference public information shared by the company. You can find more detail here: [PLATFORM_LINK].”

**D. Investor materials request**  
“We appreciate your interest. Official investor materials and company announcements are available at [IR_URL].”

**E. Rumor/speculation**  
“We do not comment on market speculation or unverified information. Please refer to Avalon’s official public disclosures and investor resources at [IR_URL].”

**F. Hostile or misleading comment**  
“Thanks for your comment. For accuracy, we encourage all stakeholders to rely on the company’s official public disclosures available at [IR_URL].”

### 5) Escalation triggers
Escalate immediately if a comment:
- asks about future share price
- asks about unannounced deals or results
- references insider information
- contains legal threats
- contains false claims about financing, approvals, or partnerships
- comes from press, analysts, regulators, or institutional investors
- creates reputational or compliance risk

### 6) Moderation rules
Hide, report, or escalate content that includes:
- impersonation
- hate speech
- harassment
- spam
- financial pumping language
- repeated false claims
- requests for nonpublic information
